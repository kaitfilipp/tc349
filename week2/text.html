Professional Bio

Kaitlyn Filipp is a creative advertising student specializing in design at Michigan State University. Her hometown is Shelby Township, Michigan but currently lives in East Lansing while she obtains her degree. She is drawn to the design industry because of the ability to be creative in her every day job. Creating things is something that she loves to do, and putting that into a career would be her ultimate dream. She is most interested in art direction and photography.

During the summer of 2012, Kaitlyn interned for Know Advertising, a small advertising agency in Royal Oak, Michigan. Here she had the opportunity to work directly with the president and creative director. She also obtained great experience in the advertising industry through creating a complete campaign for a non-profit organization and presenting it to them at the end of the internship. She also currently works for University Advancement at Michigan State University, where she is the design assistant. Here she gets to work with the designer in creating print ads, brochures, flyers, and invitations for alumni events.

Kaitlyn contributes to Michigan State University by holding the position of vice president of Spartan International Advertising Association. In her spare time, you can find Kaitlyn taking photographs, reading books or making crafts. You can find Kaitlyn online at kaitfilipp.carbonmade.com.


Panic.com Critique

The website Panic is a very well organized website. Their homepage clearly states what they do, and how to get to where you want to go on the site. The visuals are very appealing to the eye. Everything is clearly organized and nothing seems out of place or confusing. Personally, I like the simplicity of the site. What is needed to be said is said and there’s no extra information to distract the viewer.

I really enjoy how everything is organized. It’s not set up like a traditional website, which makes it interesting to me. The design is consistent throughout all of the linked pages. They clearly care about their customer’s needs. You can easily find ways to contact people for help, tutorials to help you use their programs, and organized information to help you learn about what they are offering to their customers.

The only aspect of the web page that has bothered me is the size of the text and the link images. In my opinion, everything seems kind of big, so it takes effort to scroll through and find what you need. It’s not a major issue in my mind, but I can picture that if the size of the text and the images were just a tad smaller, the website would be just a bit easier to navigate. Overall, the site is very user-friendly and caters to Panic’s customers.
